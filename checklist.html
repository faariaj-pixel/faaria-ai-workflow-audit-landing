AI Workflow Readiness Checklist

Use these 25 questions to decide which workflow is worth automating first — and which ones need more clarity before AI enters the process.

How to use it

Pick one workflow, answer each question honestly, then count how many answers are clear. The strongest automation candidates are repetitive, documented, measurable, low-risk, and owned by a clear person or team.

25 readiness questions

Assess the workflow before you automate it.

Workflow clarity

  1. Can you describe the workflow from start to finish?
  2. Is the trigger for the workflow clear?
  3. Are the steps documented anywhere?
  4. Do team members follow the same process consistently?
  5. Do you know where delays or errors usually happen?

Repetition and volume

  1. Does this workflow happen weekly or more often?
  2. Are the same decisions or messages repeated?
  3. Does it involve copying, formatting, summarizing, routing, or follow-up?
  4. Would saving time here improve client or team experience?
  5. Can you estimate the hours spent on it each month?

Data and tools

  1. Is the required information stored in predictable places?
  2. Is the information accurate enough to rely on?
  3. Do the tools involved integrate or export data cleanly?
  4. Are there privacy, compliance, or client-confidentiality constraints?
  5. Is there a human review point for sensitive outputs?

Ownership and risk

  1. Who owns the workflow today?
  2. Who would approve a change to the workflow?
  3. What could go wrong if automation made a mistake?
  4. Which steps should stay human-led?
  5. Is the team willing to update the process if needed?

Adoption and measurement

  1. What outcome would prove the automation worked?
  2. Can you measure time saved, faster turnaround, fewer errors, or better follow-up?
  3. Who needs training or documentation?
  4. Can you pilot the change with one workflow before expanding?
  5. What is the smallest useful next step?

Quick scoring guide

20–25 clear answers: strong candidate for automation planning.

14–19 clear answers: map and clean up the process first.

Under 14 clear answers: start with workflow documentation before adding AI.
