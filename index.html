The real problem

AI does not fix messy operations. It exposes them.

Your team may already be experimenting with ChatGPT, Claude, Zapier, n8n, or other workflow tools. But if the underlying process is unclear, undocumented, or owned by one key person, automation can create more confusion instead of less.

The fastest wins usually come from the boring work: follow-ups, reporting, onboarding, document preparation, handoffs, intake, and recurring administrative tasks.

FAQ

Questions before you start

Do we need to know which AI tool we want to use?

No. The audit is designed to help you identify the workflow and business problem first. Tool selection comes after clarity.

Is this for technical teams only?

No. This is built for service-based businesses and non-technical teams that want practical AI adoption support without jargon.

Will you build the automation too?

The audit identifies and prioritizes opportunities. If there is a strong fit, the next step can be done-with-you or done-for-you implementation.

What if our workflows are undocumented?

That is common. The audit helps surface the current process, clarify handoffs, and identify what needs documentation before automation.

Can you guarantee hours saved?

No guarantee is made without reviewing the workflow. The audit is designed to identify realistic improvement opportunities and what would need to be measured.
